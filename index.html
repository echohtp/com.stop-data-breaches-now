What are the best ways to minimize the pain?

Data breaches happen!

It’s one of the worst things about running a business in this day and age: despite our best efforts to safeguard data and maintain online security, data breaches happen.

No one is immune—not large corporations, not small businesses. It can happen to anyone. And when a breach occurs, it isn’t pretty. It’s exhausting, expensive, and embarrassing, not to mention disruptive to your business.

Do you have a plan in mind for when a breach occurs?

So many people think they’re prepared, but when the worst actually happens, they realize that they had no idea what they were doing. Maybe you have an incident response retainer in place; maybe you have cyber insurance. And perhaps you even have a law firm that specializes in privacy and security law, enabling you to get expert advice if your company is hacked. These are good things! But they might not be enough. While they can help you navigate the murky waters, they can’t help your business get back on track. That responsibility lies with you.

The best thing you can do for your company is this:

Develop a detailed plan for what you’ll do if a data breach occurs.

To ensure your company recovers quickly, you’ll need to plan for all the following:

  • Choose the right incident response firm for your unique scenario—Not all incident response firms are created equal, and this spells disaster for many corporations when they choose one that does not have adequate skillsets for their needs. In fact, a past client of mine worked with an incident response firm that incorrectly diagnosed a HIPAA violation when there was none, causing months of headache for this company. The right firm will specialize in your type of case, they will be experienced, and they will deploy quickly in the case of an attack. It’s your responsibility to vet firms and figure out the appropriate one for your needs. (Learn more about choosing a great incident response firm on pages 7-14.)
  • Choose a law firm that provides expert advice and support—A good law firm is indispensable in this situation, but not all law firms can handle the intricacies of a data breach. Find a law firm that specializes in privacy and security, specifically cyber breaches. Your regular lawyer probably does not see very many data breaches and therefore cannot provide the type of specialize counsel that you need. And, be certain that your insurance carrier will reimburse you for the legal expenses. It would be disastrous if you suddenly found out that your chosen law firm wasn’t covered under your cyber insurance. Unfortunately, that’s something that has happened to my past clients. (Learn more about legal counsel on page 29.)
  • Work with your cyber insurance provider to minimize expenses—First of all, make sure you have the right cyber insurance policy in place! There are so many products, and there’s no “one size fits all.” Then, you’ll need to be sure you’re actually covered in the case of a breach. Once you’re sure that you can be reimbursed for incident response, you’ll need to work with your insurance company to find a solution to the problem. (Learn how to choose the right policy and ask the right questions on page 28.)
  • Minimize disruption to your company and quickly get back to business as usual—Make it a priority to get back to work as quickly as possible after a data breach. The groundwork for this starts long before a data breach happens—in fact, you should start tomorrow. All relevant personnel in your company should know exactly what they need to do if company data is compromised. You should be training and practicing incident response actions. And, you should have playbooks for common tasks. (Learn more about all these topics on pages 22-27.)
